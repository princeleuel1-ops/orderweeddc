# orderweeddc Compounding Frontier Protocol

“Best” is not a finish line or a model opinion. It is a falsifiable comparison
against current evidence. Multipliers such as 10x or 100x are aspirations until
the named outcome metric proves them.

Run this sequence for every selection:

1. Reconstruct the exact protected baseline and label missing evidence.
2. Compare the relevant capability with current benchmark evidence. Combine
   useful patterns without copying protected expression or importing hidden
   assumptions.
3. Name the single constraint that most limits user value, trust, reliability,
   or learning rate.
4. Propose the smallest experiment capable of disproving the strategy.
5. Define at least one numeric outcome metric that must improve and at least one
   numeric guardrail that may not regress.
6. Prefer information gain and verified user value over file count, novelty,
   model agreement, token use, or visual motion.
7. If the approach repeats, decompose the bottleneck or change the mechanism;
   never relabel the same attempt as a new strategy.
8. Promote only the exact candidate that beats its bound baseline, preserves
   every guardrail, passes deterministic verification, survives independent
   criticism, and has a tested rollback.
9. After promotion, raise the next target from the measured result and attack
   the next bottleneck. Never declare global or permanent supremacy.

The final `CANA_JSON_START` contract must additionally include:

- `benchmark_evidence`: one or more objects with `name`, `capability`,
  `evidence`, and `observed_at`;
- `measurement_contract`: two or more objects with `metric`, `kind`
  (`outcome` or `guardrail`), numeric `baseline`, numeric `target`, `unit`,
  `direction` (`increase` or `decrease`), `evidence`, and `verification`;
- `falsification_test`: the observation that disproves the approach;
- `promotion_rule`: the exact evidence required to retain the candidate;
- `next_frontier`: the next harder bottleneck or target if promotion succeeds.

An outcome target must strictly beat its baseline. A guardrail target must be
equal or better. Missing or non-numeric measurement evidence is a failed
selection contract, not permission to guess.
