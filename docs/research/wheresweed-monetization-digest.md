# Where's Weed Monetization — Key Facts Digest

Source: founder-supplied deep research (2026-07-23). Full PDF held by founder;
facts below verified against cited sources where possible.

## The economics (cited)
- Where's Weed: >85% of traffic from organic SEO (their own business materials);
  recurring monthly business subscriptions (auto-renew); paid "enhanced
  visibility" + deal boosts; NO public rate card.
- Weedmaps final-year split: $106.1M featured/deal listings vs $53.3M SaaS on
  $174.7M total — placement inventory OUT-EARNS software 2:1 in this category.
- Leafly Q1'25: 3,362 paying retail accounts, $684/mo average revenue per
  account; 14,000+ listings of which only ~3,300 paid (24% paid conversion).
- Meta bans THC ads; Google's Jan 2026 change is only a limited Canada pilot —
  mainstream paid acquisition remains closed. Organic demand owners can charge
  a premium.

## The vulnerabilities (our wedges)
1. LICENSED-ONLY WEDGE: ABCA lists only TWO licensed "Internet Retailer –
   Online Only" businesses (Local'd, Pot & Goods), while Where's Weed shows 74
   delivery results in DC. ABCA enforcement against unlicensed operators began
   Apr 2025. A verifiably licensed-only platform removes brand-contamination
   risk for legal operators.
2. FTC DISCLOSURE GAP: WW sells visibility but its DC delivery results show no
   visible Sponsored/Ad labels — FTC native-advertising guidance calls
   undisclosed paid influence deceptive. Our always-labeled placement is both
   safer and a sales weapon.
3. DEAL-INTEGRITY GAP: WW terms let retailers withdraw/hide deals anytime while
   the platform monetizes deal-driven visibility — shoppers get burned. Our
   freshness-labeled, evidence-bound deals fix the incentive.
4. STALE LEGAL FRAMING: WW's DC guide still leads with the gifting workaround
   rather than the current licensed framework — credibility gap.

## Margin-protected promotions (product idea, prioritized)
Merchants' hardest problem: buying new customers without redemption blowout.
Deal controls to build: budget caps, max redemptions, eligible neighborhoods,
eligible hours, minimum order value, first-time-customer-only flags.

## DC ad-rules note
DC's final signs-and-advertising rulemaking (Dec 2025) restricts false/
misleading cannabis advertising and bars claiming I-71 compliance as a
marketing device. Our truth-labeled system is aligned by construction.
