## TIER 1 NATIONAL PLATFORMS

### 1. WEEDMAPS (weedmaps.com)

**What merchants get (from 10-K / SEC filings, commercial terms):**
- WM Listings: full listing page with live product menu, license disclosure, hours, contact info
- WM Orders: pickup + delivery order intake, connects to dispensary POS (select integrations)
- WM Store: embeddable white-label menu widget for the dispensary's own website
- WM Connectors: API/integration hub for menu sync, inventory updates, order fulfillment
- WM Insights: analytics dashboard — user engagement, traffic trends, listing performance; brand clients see market rank, retailer coverage data
- WM Ads (add-on): Featured Listings (premium placement), WM Deals (discount/promo tools), banner ads, keyword-tied banner ads
- WM Dispatch (add-on): last-mile delivery logistics software + driver apps

**Pricing (REPORTED):**
- Basic free listing exists (name/address/hours/simple menu); no paid features
- Monthly subscription ("Monthly Package") — exact tiers not public but converging industry data:
  - Entry / standard featured listing: ~$150–$500/mo (REPORTED via competitor comparison page tokehead.com, 2026)
  - Premium placement (bidding): $500–$2,000+/mo; in ultra-competitive markets (SF, LA, etc.) top spots reportedly $10,000–$100,000+/mo (REPORTED — techpos.com citing market-level data, 2022; deeproots.io case study)
  - Blended national average: ~$2,805/mo per client (PUBLISHED in final 10-K, referenced in our existing files)
- Payment: monthly in advance, auto-renews; late fee 1.5%/mo
- Contract: monthly rolling; no annual lock-in published in commercial terms, but sales-led negotiation common
- Prohibited-use clause: $10,000/mo liquidated damages if you use Weedmaps listing to promote competing platform services (PUBLISHED in commercial terms)

**Onboarding:**
- Claim or add business via weedmaps.com/business; sales-led for paid tiers; free listing self-serve
- POS connection required for live menu sync

**Merchant pain points (cited):**
- Pay-to-play visibility: stop paying, you disappear overnight (deeproots.io, 420optimized.com, nicheseoagency.com — all citing industry consensus)
- Weedmaps owns customer data: orders placed through WM platform = Weedmaps' customer relationship, not the dispensary's (deeproots.io)
- No brand equity built: SEO credit goes to weedmaps.com domain, not the merchant's site (multiple sources)
- Inventory mismatch frustrates shoppers when menus lag real stock (wereadreviews analysis; clientverge.com)
- App glitches and system reliability complaints (industry consensus)
- High fees in competitive markets destroy SMB ROI (techpos.com: "anywhere from $10,000 to $100,000 per dispensary")
- Platform risk: WM stock down 80%+ from peak; multiple layoff rounds 2022-2023 (deeproots.io)
- Prohibited-use clause is aggressive and anti-competitive (Weedmaps commercial terms)

**Contract / lock-in:**
- Month-to-month rolling; no published annual lock-in; sales reps may negotiate custom terms
- Self-serve for basic; sales-led for featured/premium

### 2. LEAFLY (business.leafly.com)

**What merchants get (from success.leafly.com, MSA, help docs — all verified 2025-2026):**
- Starter plan: customized listing with tagline + photos, competitor-ad-free listing page, auto-link menu to strain/product info pages, embeddable menu on own website, unlimited online orders (pickup + delivery), respond to customer reviews, basic map marker, limited deal postings
- Pro plan: all Starter + better placement across Leafly, unlimited deals postings, custom map marker + logo, mobile banner spotlight on dispensary list/map, maximum display time on home/explore pages, competitive analytics via Leafly Insights (market share, competitor data), access to Boosts ad product
- Leafly Ads: short-term amplification — top-5 directory placement, geotargeted hero deals, display ads on Leafly.com, mobile app placements, homepage feature
- POS integration for live menu sync
- Pre-order capability (orders for next business day before close)

**Pricing (REPORTED — Leafly does not publish rates):**
- Starter: ~$500/mo (REPORTED — dabdash.com blog, operator anecdotes)
- Pro: ~$500–$4,000+/mo including Boosts (REPORTED — dabdash.com blog; one operator reported $5,400 annual for basic listing with poor ROI; another was promised 35,000 impressions and received fewer than 100)
- Annual price increases up to 10% at renewal (PUBLISHED in MSA, updated Jan 16, 2026)
- Fees nonrefundable; late payments accrue 1.5%/mo (PUBLISHED in MSA)
- Contract: sales-led; pricing set per Quote; MSA governs

**Onboarding:**
- Submit request via success.leafly.com; regional Leafly representative contacts you with pricing and options
- Claim flow requires license verification
- AI-generated content must carry a disclaimer (PUBLISHED in MSA 2026)

**Merchant pain points (cited):**
- Low ROI vs. promises: operators report far fewer impressions than sold (dabdash.com blog citing operator accounts — ANECDOTE)
- Leafly domain gets SEO credit, not the merchant's site; iframe menus pass no SEO value to merchant (dabdash.com blog)
- Customer relationship stays with Leafly: order data, email, purchase history owned by Leafly (dabdash.com blog)
- Contract difficulties: auto-renewals, hard to cancel, customer support access complaints (Exa research citing Leafly user review patterns)
- Review authenticity concerns: users question some reviews; Leafly requires documentation to dispute competitor/ex-employee reviews (Exa research)
- Financial instability: Nasdaq non-compliance notice 2023 when market cap fell below $50M minimum; three rounds of ~21% workforce cuts 2022-2023 (dabdash.com, citing public filings)
- Pricing not disclosed publicly — merchants negotiate blind (MSA confirms "pricing per Quote")

**Contract / lock-in:**
- Sales-led; custom Quote per merchant; annual renewals with up to 10% price increase
- Arbitration-only dispute resolution; class action waiver (MSA)

### 10. DUTCHIE (dutchie.com)

**What merchants get (from Sacra research, pricingnow.com, support docs):**
- Basic: core e-commerce online ordering, embedded menus on dispensary website, inventory sync with POS
- Pro: integrated payments (Dutchie Pay / Pay-by-Bank ACH), loyalty programs, marketing automation
- Enterprise: fully custom, multi-location management, dedicated support, advanced features
- WM Store equivalent — menus embedable on merchant's own website
- Complex tiered pricing for product quantities (weight-based pricing)
- Dutchie Pay: ACH payment processing, ~25% higher average cart size claimed (from support docs); % + flat fee per transaction deducted at settlement; no monthly ACH fee
- Compliance tools; state reporting integration

**Pricing (REPORTED — not publicly published):**
- Basic e-commerce: ~$299/mo per location (REPORTED — Sacra, pricingnow.com, consistent across multiple sources)
- Comprehensive (Pro/Enterprise): ~$700–$1,000/mo per location (REPORTED)
- One-time setup fee: ~$349 (REPORTED — pricingnow.com)
- Annual price increases expected ("renewal caps")
- 3-year TCO for comprehensive plan: ~$36,000–$38,000 (REPORTED — pricingnow.com analysis)
- Funding: $400M valuation after $100M raise, Jan 2024 (Sacra)

**Onboarding:**
- Sales-led; contact form; moderate setup time for Pro/Enterprise (days for Basic, weeks for full suite)
- POS integration required for live menu sync

**Merchant pain points:**
- Not a discovery platform: Dutchie is an order-fulfillment engine, not a consumer marketplace; you still need Weedmaps/Leafly for discovery
- Pricing not fully public; hidden costs (setup, migration, training) add up
- Fee transparency issue: confusing display of service fees/delivery fees in cart can drop conversion rates (techoboll.com)
- Annual price increases not capped transparently

**Contract / lock-in:**
- Sales-led; custom contracts; migration costs create switching friction

## TOP 10 MERCHANT PAIN POINTS ACROSS ALL PLATFORMS
### (Ranked by frequency + severity — each is a conversion lever for orderweeddc)

**1. Pay-to-play visibility that disappears when you stop paying.**
Every major platform (Weedmaps, Leafly, Leafbuyer) gates visibility behind recurring fees. Stop paying = invisible. Cited across deeproots.io, 420optimized.com, nicheseoagency.com, dabdash.com. *Lever: orderweeddc gives merit-based placement with no auction.*

**2. Platform owns the customer, not the dispensary.**
Weedmaps and Leafly capture customer name, email, order history, and purchase intent — then re-market those customers to competitors. Cited at deeproots.io, dabdash.com. *Lever: orderweeddc gives merchant full access to their own customer data.*

**3. Pricing opacity — no rates published, must talk to sales.**
Weedmaps, Leafly, Where's Weed, Leafbuyer, Jane, Dutchie all hide pricing behind "contact us" walls. Creates distrust and wastes owner time. *Lever: orderweeddc publishes pricing publicly — no sales call needed.*

**4. SEO credit goes to the platform, not the merchant.**
Embedded iframes and marketplace URLs mean all product/menu content builds Weedmaps'/Leafly's domain authority, not the dispensary's. Cited at dabdash.com, 420optimized.com. *Lever: orderweeddc gives merchants a backlink and structured data that benefits their own domain.*

**5. Poor ROI measurement — no attribution, no real analytics.**
Weedmaps' basic plan provides minimal insights; Leafly locks analytics behind Pro tier. Merchants cannot measure cost-per-visit or cost-per-order. *Lever: orderweeddc provides free merchant analytics with UTM/attribution.*

**6. Inventory mismatch between listed menu and actual stock.**
Menu sync failures create customer frustration and chargebacks. Cited at wereadreviews, clientverge.com. *Lever: orderweeddc requires POS sync or manual confirmation; real-time availability flag.*

**7. Annual contract lock-in + aggressive renewal terms.**
Leafly's MSA allows up to 10% annual price increases with 30-day notice only; nonrefundable fees; arbitration-only dispute resolution. Merchants trapped. *Lever: orderweeddc offers month-to-month with published terms.*

**8. Review manipulation and fake review vulnerability.**
Both Weedmaps and Leafly face allegations of fake reviews; dispute processes are burdensome. Cited in Exa research on Leafly complaints; Weedmaps community discussions. *Lever: orderweeddc uses staff-verified reviews with ABCA license tie-back.*

**9. No loyalty tools — platform benefits, not merchant.**
Most directories (Weedmaps, Leafly Starter, Where's Weed, AllBud, CannaPages) offer zero loyalty features. Leafbuyer and Jane offer them but at added cost or complexity. *Lever: orderweeddc includes merchant-branded loyalty in base tier.*

**10. Platform financial instability creates business continuity risk.**
Leafly Nasdaq non-compliance; Weedmaps stock -80% from peak; multiple layoff rounds both platforms 2022-2024. Merchants fear their primary customer acquisition channel may disappear. Cited at dabdash.com, deeproots.io. *Lever: orderweeddc is DC-local, bootstrapped, and not dependent on VC runway.*
