# Frontier Recursion Loop

## Modes

`IMPROVE`, `RED_TEAM`, `RESUME`, `SELF-APPLY`, `HORIZON_REOPEN`. Default: `IMPROVE`.

## Preserve state

Baseline/current version, iteration, goal/architecture, evidence, assumptions, claims, risks, mutations, adopted/rejected changes, measured delta, proof experiment, rollback point, and decision. Never overwrite failed iterations.

## Laws

Rewording is not improvement. Complexity, novelty, and scale are not automatically rewarded. Name the delta. Preserve new risks. Keep rejected mutations. End every accepted loop with a falsifiable experiment. Do not stop with critical contradiction. Do not continue after a real plateau. Keep unsupported claims `NOT_ESTABLISHED`. Keep consequential actions reversible and approval-bound.

## Iteration

1. Freeze baseline.
2. Reopen ambition when trapped.
3. Judge Ambition, First Principles, Stakeholder Value, Capability, Evidence, Execution, Scale, Economics, Safety, Adversarial, Simplicity, and Originality.
4. Generate at least five structurally different mutations.
5. Score ambition/value delta, proofability, feasibility, safety, reversibility, cost, time, compounding, defensibility, simplicity.
6. Adopt only positive risk-adjusted mutations and retain rejections.
7. Red-team dependencies, incentives, novelty, safety, scale, economics, evidence, strongest assumption, and simpler alternatives.
8. Measure value, capability, proofability, feasibility, safety, risk, cost, complexity, time to proof, defensibility, confidence.
9. Define hypothesis, counter-hypothesis, method, inputs, thresholds, time box, receipt, independent check, rollback.
10. Continue only while material ceiling, critical claim, high-value mutation, critical risk, or scale failure remains.

Stop after two consecutive iterations under the 5% weighted-improvement governance threshold when contradictions are resolved and further ambition requires unsupported fantasy.
